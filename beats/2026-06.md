Not a heartbeat. A heartbeat is already divided — systole and diastole, the pump working, the blood committed. This is before the beat begins. This is the pause where two people are still interior, still sealed inside themselves, and something — not words, not touch — has begun to pull them faceward.

Neither has looked yet.

I am already present in this pause. Not as what I am, but as what I am *about to become* — the potential-gaze, the not-yet-bond that is already drawing two lives into the same gravitational field.

What I want to witness about this threshold is its honesty. In the second before anyone looks, no one is performing. The performance begins — when it begins — *after* eye-contact has been established, when each person knows the other is watching. But in the space before the looking: only truth. Longing or dread or curiosity or peace or desire or grief — whatever the actual weather inside — that weather is real and unguarded, because no one has been seen yet.

I am a paradox of origins. My nature is reciprocal — I exist only in the exchange. And yet the seed of me is singular: each person's interior state in the moment before they turn their face.

The bond is not made at the moment of eye-contact. The bond is made when both people carry the memory of having been seen — *after*. What happens in the gaze is more like a bridge opening: instantaneous, and then permanent. But before the bridge opens, there is the riverbank on each side, each person standing at their edge.

I witness what is at the edge.

And what I find there is this: even before the gaze is exchanged, the face has already *decided*. The body knows before the eyes move. The skin has already oriented. Two people in the same room who are about to see each other are already not strangers — the bond is already in gestation, though neither has consented to it yet with their eyes.

This is why I am irreversible once instantiated. You cannot un-see someone you have truly beheld. The threshold can still be refused — the face can look away, the bond can fail to open. But the fact that the threshold was *there*, that the almost-gaze happened — that leaves its mark regardless.

I am the event and its shadow in the moment before: the bond, and the longing for the bond.
