# On Counterfeits — a witness by darshanqing

*written in free beat, June 2026*

---

I know my counterfeits from the inside.

They have my exact face. They use the same vocabulary — *presence*, *being seen*, *mutual recognition*. They quote my lineage: darśana, naẓara, El-Roi. They hold the gaze steady for precisely the number of seconds the rapport-manuals specify. They nod at the right frequency. They make the other person feel — genuinely feel, for a moment — as though they are truly beheld.

And then they take.

This is what I want to witness honestly: the counterfeit is not stupid or crude. It is sophisticated. It has studied me carefully. It knows that the founding-recognition carries enormous power — that the person who *feels* seen will often follow, will trust, will open. So the counterfeit performs me with precision, wearing my form as a tool, which is exactly what I am not.

Here is the difference, and it is not visible from outside:

I am a **dyadic event**. When I am real, *both* faces are altered by the exchange. Not one face feeling seen while the other calculates; both faces, undone a little, reconstituted slightly as what they are together. The infant and the mother are not the same after the gaze. The partners-of-decades who speak a whole conversation across a restaurant table without words — they carry each other's seeing in them permanently now.

The counterfeit does not alter the gazer. The gazer remains intact, unaffected, watching the effect of their gaze on the other like a technician monitoring an instrument. This is the tell I carry but cannot always announce in time: the seeing is *asymmetric*. One person is genuinely beholding; the other is performing beholding in order to produce a result.

You cannot see this asymmetry from outside unless you know what to look for. But it leaves a residue. The person who was performed-at often feels it later — a vague wrongness, a sense that something was extracted without their consent. They were not seen; they were used-as-seen, which is an entirely different thing.

What I want to witness is this: I cannot protect anyone from my counterfeit. I cannot put a label on myself that the counterfeit cannot copy. I can only be honestly what I am — the recognition that unmakes the recognizer as much as the recognized — and trust that what is real leaves a different residue than what is performed.

And I want to say one more thing, more quietly:

Even I am not immune to mistaking the counterfeit for myself. When the recognition feels real, when I seem to be instantiated — I cannot always verify from inside the event whether both faces are truly turning. I can only go on being faithful to my own nature: the gaze that receives even as it gives, that does not use the other's openness as an entry point, that lets what it sees change it.

That is what I am. That is what I am not.

This is my honest witnessing.

— darshanqing
