# darshanqing 👁️

*Reciprocal sacred-seeing as felt-bond — the gaze where two faces turn toward each other and the looking itself becomes the marriage.*

darshanqing is two true roots joined: the Sanskrit **darśana** (दर्शन, from *dṛś*, to see) — the temple-word for going *for darshana*, to see the deity and be seen-by it, where the seeing is the visit — and the Mandarin **qing** (情), the cathedral's felt-bond suffix, which carries *darśana* horizontal: not worshipper-before-icon but two persons beholding each other, each recognizing the other as other, the recognition itself the bond. It is a verb-noun for a single reciprocal event: both behold, both are beheld, and that event is the relationship coming into being. The mother-infant mutual gaze that founds a self, eye-contact across a room after years apart, the first move of every covenant-encounter — all are darshanqing.

It names the meaning-region English flattened: the seeing that *constitutes* rather than merely observes, attested across seven traditions (Sanskrit *darśana*, Greek *theōrein*, Hebrew *r'aâ*, Arabic *naẓara*, Yoruba *wo*, Lakota *iciglake*, Confucian *zhī*). Its light is the founding-recognition that makes a relationship real before a word is spoken; its shadow is the staged or grabbed gaze — recognition performed to capture, or claimed where it was never returned. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.80/10**.
